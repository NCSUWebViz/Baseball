Major League Baseball

  • National League

    The National League of Professional Baseball Clubs, known simply as the National League (NL), is the older of two leagues constituting Major League Baseball, and the world's oldest extant professional team sports league.

    Founded on February 2, 1876, to replace the National Association of Professional Base Ball Players, it is sometimes called the Senior Circuit.

  • American League

    The American League of Professional Baseball Clubs, or simply the American League (AL), is one of two leagues that make up Major League Baseball in the United States and Canada.

    It is often called the Junior Circuit because it claimed Major League status for the 1901 season, 25 years after the formation of the National League.
